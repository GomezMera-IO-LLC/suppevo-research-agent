## Executive Summary

Maria's genome shows a **moderate overall diabetes risk** with a specific pattern:

1. **Beta-cell function:** Mostly protected at the major loci (TCF7L2 protective, SLC30A8 protective), with moderate vulnerability at KCNJ11, KCNQ1, CDKN2A/B, and GCK
2. **Obesity/Weight:** **High genetic risk** via FTO (heterozygous at 5 SNPs) — predisposes to weight gain, which is the primary modifiable driver of T2D
3. **Insulin resistance:** Moderate — ADIPOQ variants + TNF-alpha pro-inflammatory genotype
4. **Metabolic inflammation:** Active — TNF-alpha AG genotype promotes low-grade chronic inflammation
5. **GCKR:** Protective CC genotype (unlike Carlos) — no fructose-driven metabolic vulnerability

**The dominant risk pattern for Maria is: FTO-driven obesity tendency → insulin resistance → eventual beta-cell exhaustion.** The pathway to prevention is weight management and anti-inflammatory support.

### 3. KCNJ11 — Potassium Channel (Beta-Cell) | Chromosome 11

| SNP | Genotype | Risk Allele | Impact |
|-----|----------|-------------|--------|
| **rs5219** (E23K) | **CT** | T | **Heterozygous risk — mildly impaired insulin secretion** |

**Interpretation:** The KCNJ11 E23K variant (T allele) encodes a gain-of-function mutation in the Kir6.2 subunit of the ATP-sensitive potassium channel in pancreatic beta-cells. This makes the channel slightly more active, requiring higher ATP levels to close → less insulin released per glucose stimulus. Maria is heterozygous — mild effect (OR ~1.15 per allele).

Note: Sulfonylurea drugs (glipizide, glimepiride) directly target this channel. If Maria ever needed diabetes medication, sulfonylureas would be pharmacogenomically appropriate for her genotype.

**Reference:** Song J et al. *BMC Med Genet.* 2017. [PMID: 28587604](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28587604/)

| **rs13266634** | **CC** | C | **Homozygous risk — reduced zinc transport in beta-cells** |

**Interpretation:** SLC30A8 encodes ZnT8, the zinc transporter responsible for zinc accumulation in insulin secretory granules. Zinc is essential for insulin crystallization, storage, and secretion. **Practical implication:** Adequate zinc intake is more important for Maria than for someone with CT or TT genotype.

## The Diabetes Pathway for Maria

Based on her genetics, the most likely route to diabetes would be:

```
FTO-driven weight gain tendency
    → Visceral fat accumulation
    → TNF-alpha AG amplifies adipose inflammation (more per kg gained)
    → ADIPOQ CG reduces protective adiponectin
    → Progressive insulin resistance
    → Beta-cells compensate by producing more insulin
    → KCNJ11 CT + KCNQ1 CT + SLC30A8 CC + CDKN2A/B CT
       = Mildly impaired beta-cell reserve
    → Eventually beta-cells cannot keep up
    → Fasting glucose rises → Prediabetes → T2D
```

**Critical point:** This is a PREVENTABLE cascade. The genetic entry point is FTO-driven weight gain. If weight is managed, the downstream cascade doesn't activate regardless of the beta-cell variants.

## Recommendations

### Tier 1: Weight Management (Addresses FTO + Primary Risk Driver)

Maria's FTO genotype means she has a stronger biological drive toward appetite and caloric surplus. This is NOT a willpower issue — it's a satiety signaling difference. Strategies:

- **Protein-forward meals** — protein has the strongest satiety effect per calorie; aim for 25-30g protein per meal
- **Fiber-rich foods** — slows gastric emptying, extends satiety; goal 25-30g/day
- **Regular meal timing** — avoid long gaps that trigger overeating
- **Resistance training** — increases lean mass, improves insulin sensitivity independently of weight loss
- **Sleep 7-8 hours** — sleep deprivation upregulates hunger hormones (ghrelin) and worsens FTO effects

### Tier 2: Anti-Inflammatory Support (Addresses TNF-alpha + ADIPOQ)

Maria's inflammatory genotype means weight gain creates MORE insulin resistance per unit of fat gained:

| Supplement/Approach | Mechanism | Dose |
|-------------------|-----------|------|
| **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** | Directly suppresses TNF-alpha and NF-kB; resolves adipose inflammation | 2-3g combined EPA+DHA/day |
| **Curcumin** (with piperine) | Inhibits NF-kB → reduces TNF-alpha transcription | 500-1000 mg/day |
| **Magnesium** | Anti-inflammatory; improves insulin signaling; most people deficient | 200-400 mg/day |
| **Vitamin D** | Supports beta-cell insulin secretion; reduces inflammation; DHCR7 GG suggests lower production | 2000-4000 IU/day (test 25-OH-D level) |
| **Exercise** — even moderate | Reduces TNF-alpha, increases adiponectin (counters ADIPOQ variant) | 150 min/week moderate |

### Tier 3: Beta-Cell Support (Addresses SLC30A8 + KCNJ11 + CDKN2A/B)

| Supplement/Approach | Mechanism | Dose |
|-------------------|-----------|------|
| **Zinc** | SLC30A8 CC means less efficient zinc transport → needs adequate substrate | 15-30 mg/day (zinc picolinate or citrate) |
| **Chromium** | Enhances insulin receptor sensitivity | 200-400 mcg/day (chromium picolinate) |
| **Berberine** (if prediabetic) | Activates AMPK, improves glucose uptake, reduces hepatic glucose output | 500 mg 2-3x/day with meals |
| **Alpha-lipoic acid** | Antioxidant protecting beta-cells from oxidative stress | 300-600 mg/day |

### Tier 4: Monitoring

| Marker | Target | Frequency |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Fasting glucose** | < 100 mg/dL | Annually (more if elevated) |
| **HbA1c** | < 5.7% | Annually |
| **Fasting insulin / HOMA-IR** | Insulin < 10 μIU/mL | Annually (early warning) |
| **hs-CRP** | < 1.0 mg/L | Annually (inflammation marker) |
| **25-OH Vitamin D** | 40-60 ng/mL | Once, then annually |
| **Adiponectin** (optional) | Higher is better | Consider if insulin resistant |
| **Weight / Waist circumference** | Stable or declining | Monthly |
