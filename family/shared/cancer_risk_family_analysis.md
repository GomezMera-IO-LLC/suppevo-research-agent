### 5. TP53 PATHWAY — Tumor Suppression

| SNP | Gene | Risk | Carlos | Maria | Julie | Natalie | Valerie | Beatriz |
|-----|------|------|--------|-------|-------|---------|---------|---------|
| rs1042522 | TP53 Arg72Pro | C (Pro) | CG | **CC** | **CC** | CG | **CC** | CG |

**Key Findings:**
- **Maria, Julie, and Valerie are CC (Pro/Pro)** at the TP53 codon 72 polymorphism.
- The Pro72 variant produces a TP53 protein with altered apoptotic function — it's better at cell-cycle arrest but less efficient at inducing apoptosis compared to the Arg72 variant.
- This is context-dependent: Pro/Pro may increase risk for some cancers (those requiring efficient apoptosis to eliminate damaged cells) while being neutral or even protective in others.
- Julie inherited CC from Maria — both copies are Pro72.

**Recommendation:** Not actionable as a standalone finding, but it's a relevant modifier — in the context of other risk alleles, the reduced apoptotic efficiency means damaged cells may survive longer before being eliminated. Supports the importance of antioxidant defense and DNA repair optimization.

### 2. Melanoma — Carlos & Valerie (Highest Risk)

**Who:** Carlos (ASIP CC + MC1R R151C CT), Valerie (same combination)  
**Supporting:** Julie, Natalie, Beatriz all have ASIP CC

The MC1R R151C variant is one of the strongest melanoma risk alleles known (OR ~2.4). Combined with ASIP CC, Carlos and Valerie have meaningfully elevated melanoma susceptibility.

**Action:**
- Annual full-body skin examination by dermatologist (Carlos and Valerie)
- Self-examination monthly for new/changing moles (ABCDE criteria)
- Rigorous sun protection: SPF 50+ broad-spectrum, protective clothing, avoid peak UV hours
- All family members (ASIP CC): sunscreen habit, avoid tanning
- Consider baseline mole mapping for Carlos and Valerie

### 3. Julie's Thyroid + Colorectal Combination

**Who:** Julie specifically  
**Variants:** Thyroid rs944289 TT (homo) + Colorectal (8q24 GG + BMP4 CC)

Julie has the highest concentration of homozygous risk alleles in the family (8 total). Her thyroid cancer variant is unique (homozygous from both parents contributing T). Combined with her ASD and the importance of thyroid function for neurodevelopment, thyroid monitoring is important.

**Action:**
- Routine thyroid palpation during pediatric visits
- If thyroid nodules detected: lower threshold for ultrasound/biopsy given genetic background
- Annual TSH/free T4 reasonable given intersection of thyroid cancer genetics + ASD

### 4. Beatriz's MTHFR AA — DNA Repair Vulnerability

**Who:** Beatriz  
**Variant:** MTHFR C677T AA (homozygous) — ~70% reduced enzyme activity

This is the most impactful single finding for Beatriz. Severely reduced MTHFR function means:
- Impaired DNA methylation → epigenetic instability
- Reduced thymidylate synthesis → increased uracil misincorporation in DNA → strand breaks
- Elevated homocysteine → cardiovascular risk
- Increased colorectal and breast cancer risk

**Action:**
- **Methylfolate supplementation** (NOT folic acid — she can't convert it efficiently): 400-800 mcg L-methylfolate/day
- B12 (methylcobalamin form): 1000 mcg/day
- B6 (P5P form): 25-50 mg/day
- Riboflavin (B2): supports MTHFR enzyme function — 25 mg/day
- Monitor homocysteine levels
- Adequate dietary folate from leafy greens

### 5. Carlos's Prostate Cancer Risk

**Who:** Carlos  
**Variants:** KLK3/PSA GG (homo) + 4 additional hetero risk alleles

The KLK3 GG genotype is particularly important because it means Carlos's PSA levels may be constitutively lower. A "normal" PSA in him might actually represent an abnormal state — the threshold for concern should be lower.

## What This Analysis CANNOT Tell You

1. **High-penetrance mutations** — True pathogenic BRCA1/2 mutations, Lynch syndrome (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2), Li-Fraumeni (TP53 frameshift/nonsense), and familial adenomatous polyposis (APC truncating variants) are NOT detectable on the 23andMe v5 chip. If there is a strong family history of early-onset cancer (especially breast < 50, colon < 45, or multiple cancer types), clinical genetic testing (e.g., Invitae, Color Genomics panel) should be considered.

2. **Somatic mutations** — This analysis covers germline (inherited) variants only. Cancers arise from accumulated somatic mutations over a lifetime.

3. **Gene-environment interaction** — Genetic risk is modulated by lifestyle (diet, smoking, sun exposure, exercise, toxin exposure). A high genetic risk with excellent lifestyle may never manifest; a low genetic risk with poor lifestyle still can.

4. **Absolute risk** — These variants modify relative risk (OR 1.1-2.4 per allele). Most people with these risk alleles never develop cancer. They indicate where to direct screening attention, not predict outcomes.
