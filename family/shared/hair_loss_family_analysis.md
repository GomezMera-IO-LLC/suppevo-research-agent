## Key Findings by Pathway

### 1. DHT Production — SRD5A2 (5-Alpha Reductase Type 2)

This enzyme converts testosterone into DHT (dihydrotestosterone) — the hormone that miniaturizes hair follicles in androgenetic alopecia.

| SNP | Variant | Carlos Papa | CarlosG | Maria | Natalie | Valerie | Julie | Beatriz |
|-----|---------|-------------|---------|-------|---------|---------|-------|---------|
| **rs523349** | V89L | **CC (homo)** | **CC (homo)** | CG | CG | **CC (homo)** | CG | CG |

**CC genotype = Valine at position 89 = HIGHER 5-alpha reductase activity = MORE DHT produced**

- **Carlos Papa + CarlosG + Valerie: CC (homozygous)** — produce significantly more DHT from testosterone than CG or GG carriers. This is the direct mechanism of male pattern baldness. For Valerie, this means she has higher DHT production capacity — relevant if she develops female pattern hair loss (especially post-menopause when estrogen declines).
- **Maria, Natalie, Julie, Beatriz: CG (heterozygous)** — intermediate DHT production.

**This is the most actionable finding.** DHT-driven hair loss responds to:
- 5-alpha reductase inhibitors (finasteride, dutasteride — prescription)
- Natural DHT blockers (saw palmetto, pumpkin seed oil, stinging nettle root)

**Interpretation:** The 20p11 locus regulates hair follicle sensitivity to androgens (likely through PAX1/FOXA2 transcription factors that control follicle stem cell maintenance). Homozygous carriers have follicles that respond MORE to DHT — they miniaturize faster.

### 3. Vitamin D Receptor (VDR) — Hair Follicle Cycling

VDR is essential for hair follicle cycling. Without functional VDR signaling, follicles fail to enter anagen (growth phase).

| SNP | Carlos Papa | CarlosG | Maria | Natalie | Valerie | Julie | Beatriz |
|-----|-------------|---------|-------|---------|---------|-------|---------|
| rs1544410 (BsmI) | CC | CT | CC | CT | CT | CT | CT |
| rs731236 (TaqI) | AA | AG | AA | AG | AG | AG | AG |
| rs7975232 (ApaI) | AC | AC | CC | AC | AC | AC | **AA (homo)** |

- **Beatriz: VDR ApaI AA (homozygous risk)** — reduced VDR expression/activity. Her hair follicle cycling is more dependent on adequate vitamin D levels. If vitamin D is low, her hair follicles are more likely to stay in telogen (resting phase) → shedding.
- **CarlosG, Natalie, Valerie, Julie:** VDR ApaI AC (heterozygous) — moderate VDR sensitivity. Adequate vitamin D matters for hair maintenance.
- **Carlos Papa, Maria:** VDR ApaI AC/CC — varying but not maximal risk.

**Reference:** Guan L et al. "VDR signaling in hair follicle health and alopecia." *J Cell Commun Signal.* 2026. [PMID: 41473843](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41473843/)
