## Estrogen Protection Factor

All three daughters currently benefit from **estrogen-mediated uricosuric effect** — estrogen promotes renal uric acid excretion by modulating URAT1 and other transporters. This is why gout and hyperuricemia are far more common in men and post-menopausal women.

| Life Stage | Effect on Uric Acid |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Pre-menopausal | Estrogen suppresses serum urate by ~1-2 mg/dL vs. equivalent male genotype |
| Peri-menopausal | Protection gradually declines |
| Post-menopausal | Risk approaches male equivalent — genetic predisposition fully expressed |

**Implication:** Julie and Valerie especially should be aware that their genetic risk will become clinically relevant as estrogen declines. Proactive dietary habits (low fructose, hydration, coffee) established now will provide protection later.
