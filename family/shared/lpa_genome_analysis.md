## Recommended Next Steps

1. **Get Lp(a) measured** via a blood test if not already done — a one-time measurement is sufficient since Lp(a) is largely genetically determined and stable over a lifetime.
2. **Discuss with a cardiologist** — particularly regarding aggressive management of other modifiable risk factors (LDL-C, blood pressure, lifestyle).
3. **Monitor emerging therapies** — antisense oligonucleotides targeting LPA (e.g., pelacarsen, olpasiran) are in late-stage clinical trials and may become available for high-Lp(a) individuals.
