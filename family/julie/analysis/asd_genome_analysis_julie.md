# ASD-Associated Genetic Variant Analysis: Julie Gomez

## Overview

This analysis compares Julie Gomez's genome against four non-ASD family members (Carlos Gomez — father, Maria del Pilar Mera — mother, Natalie Gomez — sibling, Valerie Gomez — sibling) to identify genetic variants associated with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) that may be unique to or differentially expressed in Julie.

**Important Disclaimer:** This is an informational analysis based on published research associations, not a clinical diagnosis. ASD is a complex polygenic condition influenced by hundreds of genetic variants and environmental factors. Individual SNPs confer small increases in risk and should not be interpreted as deterministic.

---

## Key Findings Summary

Julie carries a distinct combination of homozygous risk-associated genotypes across several pathways implicated in ASD — particularly in methylation/folate metabolism, dopamine signaling, stress response, and synaptic function. While individual variants are common in the general population, the convergence of multiple homozygous risk alleles distinguishes her from her non-ASD family members.

| rs1801133 (C677T) | MTHFR | **GG (TT)** | AG (CT) | AG (CT) | AG (CT) | AG (CT) |
| rs1801131 (A1298C) | MTHFR | TT (AA) | TT (AA) | TT (AA) | TT (AA) | TT (AA) |

**Interpretation:** Julie is **homozygous** for the MTHFR C677T variant (reported as GG on the opposite strand = TT on the coding strand). Both parents are heterozygous carriers, and both siblings are also heterozygous. Julie is the only family member with the homozygous TT genotype, which reduces MTHFR enzyme activity by approximately 70%. This variant impairs folate metabolism and methylation capacity — processes critical for neurodevelopment. Multiple studies have associated homozygous MTHFR C677T with increased ASD risk (Boris et al., 2004; Pu et al., 2013 meta-analysis).

**Significance: HIGH — Unique to Julie in this family.**

## Pathway Summary

Julie's unique variant profile suggests convergent effects on several ASD-relevant pathways:

### Methylation & Epigenetic Regulation
- **MTHFR C677T homozygous** — significantly impaired methylation capacity. This affects DNA methylation, neurotransmitter synthesis (via SAMe), and detoxification. This is the single most distinctive finding.

### GABAergic Signaling (Excitatory/Inhibitory Balance)
- **rs1014531 AA** at ABAT region — unique homozygosity may affect GABA degradation, potentially disrupting the excitatory/inhibitory balance that is a core neurobiological finding in ASD research.

### Dopamine Signaling
- **rs6269 AA** within COMT — creates a specific high-activity haplotype context that may reduce dopamine availability in the prefrontal cortex, affecting executive function, attention, and reward processing.

### Synaptic Function
- **rs3746544 GT** at SNAP25 — inherited G allele from mother affects a protein critical for neurotransmitter release. Not carried by siblings.

## Actionable Considerations

Based on these genetic findings, the following areas may warrant discussion with Julie's healthcare providers:

1. **Methylation support** — Given homozygous MTHFR C677T, methylfolate (5-MTHF) rather than folic acid may be more bioavailable. B12 (methylcobalamin) status is also relevant.

2. **GABA/Excitatory-Inhibitory balance** — The ABAT variant may contribute to neurotransmitter imbalances. Magnesium, taurine, and other GABA-supportive nutrients could be explored.

3. **Dopamine optimization** — With high COMT activity (rs4680 Val/Val + rs6269 AA), Julie may have lower baseline prefrontal dopamine. This has implications for attention, executive function, and motivation.

4. **Omega-3/DHA** — While FADS genes are not uniquely affected, adequate DHA intake supports the synaptic function pathway where SNAP25 is involved.

5. **Vitamin D** — Important for neurodevelopment and immune modulation; monitor levels given the immune-related HLA findings.

## Limitations

- This analysis covers common SNPs available on the 23andMe v5 chip. Rare variants, copy number variations (CNVs), and de novo mutations — which account for a significant proportion of ASD genetic risk — are not detectable with this data.
- Several well-known ASD SNPs (rs7794745/CNTNAP2, rs1858830/MET, rs2710102/CNTNAP2, rs25531/SLC6A4) were not genotyped on this chip version.
- ASD is highly polygenic — hundreds of common variants each contribute tiny effect sizes. This analysis focuses on variants with the strongest published associations.
- Environmental factors, epigenetics, gene-gene interactions, and timing of exposures during development are not captured by genotype data alone.
- This is not a clinical genetic report and should not be used for medical decision-making without professional genetic counseling.
