## Important Notes

1. **Start one supplement at a time** (every 3–5 days) so you can identify which ones she responds well to and which may cause side effects.

2. **Kid-friendly forms matter.** Liquids, gummies, and chewables increase compliance. JoySpring MethylBee and Nordic Naturals products are designed with kids in mind.

3. **Avoid folic acid.** When purchasing ANY multivitamin or fortified food, check labels for "folic acid" vs. "methylfolate" or "5-MTHF." Julie's MTHFR status means folic acid may be counterproductive.

4. **Third-party testing matters.** Look for NSF, USP, ConsumerLab, or IFOS (for fish oils) certifications. Thorne, Nordic Naturals, and Pure Encapsulations consistently pass independent testing.

5. **Store properly.** Fish oils and probiotics need refrigeration. Most others are fine at room temperature away from heat/light.

6. **These are not substitutes for medical care.** These recommendations complement — not replace — behavioral therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy, and any medications prescribed by Julie's physician.
