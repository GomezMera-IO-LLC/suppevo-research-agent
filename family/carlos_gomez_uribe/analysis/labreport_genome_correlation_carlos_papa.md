## Understanding His Speech/Language Symptoms

His symptoms — word-finding difficulty (anomia), repetition, staying quiet, incoherent conversation — reflect damage to specific brain circuits:

### What's Happening

```
Language production requires:
1. Semantic retrieval (finding the right word in memory) — Temporal lobe
2. Phonological encoding (assembling the sounds) — Parietal/Temporal junction
3. Motor speech planning (executing the word) — Frontal lobe (Broca's area)
4. Working memory (holding the sentence together) — Prefrontal cortex

His damage pattern (APOE ε4 + diabetes + vascular):
- Temporal lobe plaques → can't retrieve words (anomia)
- White matter disconnection (vascular) → circuits don't communicate
- Prefrontal hypoperfusion → loses track mid-sentence → repetition
- Cholinergic deficit → all of the above worsened (acetylcholine is the "signal clarity" neurotransmitter)

He stays quiet because TRYING to speak is frustrating and exhausting when these circuits fail.
```

### Why He Repeats

Repetition (perseveration) occurs because:
- The last successfully retrieved word/phrase is still "loaded" in working memory
- He can't retrieve a NEW word, so the system defaults to the last one that worked
- Frontal lobe damage reduces the ability to INHIBIT the previous response
