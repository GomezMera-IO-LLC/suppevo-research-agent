**Three homozygous risk alleles at prostate cancer loci:**

- **HNF1B rs4430796 AA** — Hepatocyte Nuclear Factor 1-beta. Associated with prostate cancer via hormonal regulation and prostate tissue differentiation. Homozygous AA confers the highest risk at this locus (OR ~1.4 for homo vs. GG). CarlosG inherited one A allele from Papa → AG.

- **MSMB rs10993994 TT** — Microseminoprotein-beta. This gene encodes PSP94, a prostate secretory protein. The TT genotype causes markedly reduced MSMB expression, which is associated with aggressive prostate cancer development. **This is one of the strongest prostate cancer risk alleles.** CarlosG inherited one T → CT (moderate risk).

- **KLK3 rs2735839 GG** — PSA gene. As noted in CarlosG's analysis, this variant means PSA levels may be constitutively lower, masking developing tumors. Papa passed this to CarlosG (who is also GG).

**Combined prostate risk:** Carlos Papa has 3 homozygous + 1 heterozygous risk alleles at prostate loci. This is a **very high cumulative genetic burden** for prostate cancer.

**Critical Recommendation:** 
- If not already being screened: **immediate PSA + DRE referral**
- Discuss the KLK3 GG + MSMB TT combination with urologist — PSA threshold for biopsy should be lowered (consider 2.0-2.5 rather than standard 4.0)
- Consider prostate MRI as baseline if age-appropriate
- Annual screening minimum

### 3. LUNG CANCER — UNIQUE FAMILY SIGNAL

| SNP | Gene | Risk Allele | Carlos Papa | CarlosG | Beatriz |
|-----|------|-------------|-------------|---------|---------|
| rs1051730 | CHRNA3 | T | **AG** | GG | GG |
| rs8034191 | 15q25 | C | **CT** | TT | TT |

**Carlos Papa is the ONLY family member carrying risk alleles at both major lung cancer loci:**

- **CHRNA3 rs1051730 AG** — Nicotinic acetylcholine receptor alpha-3 subunit. The A allele (note: reported as G on opposite strand in some chips — the risk allele is the one associated with increased lung cancer/nicotine dependence). This variant is the strongest genetic predictor of both lung cancer AND nicotine addiction.

- **15q25 rs8034191 CT** — The same chromosome 15q25 nicotinic receptor cluster. Heterozygous risk.

**Important context:** These variants increase lung cancer risk BOTH through nicotine addiction (increased likelihood of smoking and difficulty quitting) AND through direct effects on lung epithelium independent of smoking. Even in never-smokers, the 15q25 locus modestly increases lung cancer susceptibility.

**No other family member carries risk alleles at either locus** — CarlosG, Beatriz, and all granddaughters are GG/TT (protected). Papa did NOT pass these to CarlosG.

**Recommendation:**
- If Papa smokes or ever smoked: **low-dose CT screening** per USPSTF guidelines
- If never-smoker: the risk is modest but worth noting — avoid secondhand smoke, occupational exposures
- The positive: he did not pass these alleles to CarlosG or grandchildren
