## Crystal Dissolution Science

### Key Principle

Urate crystals (monosodium urate / MSU) dissolve **passively** when serum uric acid is maintained below the saturation threshold (~6.8 mg/dL, ideally <6 mg/dL) for a sustained period. No supplement directly "pulls" stored uric acid from tissues — the mechanism is indirect:

1. Lower circulating serum urate below saturation
2. Maintain that level long enough for deposits to passively dissolve
3. The kidneys excrete the freed urate

### The "Dirty Dish" Hypothesis

**Reference:** Perez-Ruiz et al., 2011 — [PMID 21898351](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21898351/)

- Maintaining serum urate <6 mg/dL for 5+ years dissolved crystal deposits (tophi)
- After dissolution, serum urate only needs to stay below ~7 mg/dL to prevent re-formation
- "More effort is required to clean a dirty dish than to keep it clean"

### Imaging Confirmation

**Reference:** Modjinou et al., 2017 — [PMID 28623421](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28623421/)

- Dual-energy CT (DECT) imaging showed **100% crystal resolution** when serum urate maintained <0.5 mg/dL for 6 months
- Some physical swelling persists after crystals dissolve (non-urate components remain)
- Deposits may fluctuate in size during dissolution — can appear to enlarge before shrinking

### Timeline for Stored Urate Removal

| Deposit Size | Time to Dissolve | Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Small deposits | 6–12 months | Maintain serum urate < 6 mg/dL |
| Moderate tophi | 1–3 years | Consistent daily supplementation |
| Large tophi | 3–5+ years | May need pharmaceutical support |

## Two Pathways to Lower Uric Acid

| Pathway | How it Works | Best Ingredients |
|---|---|---|
| **Reduce production** (XO inhibition) | Block xanthine oxidase, the enzyme that creates uric acid from purines | Folic Acid, Tart Cherry (anthocyanins), Quercetin |
| **Increase excretion** (Uricosuric) | Compete with urate for renal tubular reabsorption, pushing more out through urine | Vitamin C ([PMID 835593](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/835593/)) |

## Recommended Daily Protocol

```
├── Folic Acid: 800 mcg/day (production inhibitor — strongest evidence, #1 ranked)
├── Tart Cherry Extract: 500 mg/day (XO inhibitor + anti-inflammatory)
├── Vitamin C: 500 mg/day (uricosuric — increases kidney excretion)
└── Adequate hydration: 2-3L water/day (supports renal clearance)
```

### Why This Combination

- **Folic Acid** attacks production — ranked #1 out of 13 dietary supplements tested in a network meta-analysis
- **Tart Cherry** provides dual benefit — XO inhibition PLUS anti-inflammatory (reduced CRP by 23%)
- **Vitamin C** attacks excretion — uricosuric mechanism confirmed since 1977 (PMID 835593)
- All three target different pathways, making them complementary

## Folic Acid vs Metafolin for Uric Acid

| | Folic Acid | Metafolin (5-MTHF) |
|---|---|---|
| **Form** | Synthetic, oxidized | Active, methylated |
| **Requires conversion?** | Yes (4 enzymatic steps) | No — already usable |
| **MTHFR gene issue** | 40% of people have slower conversion | Bypasses the problem |
| **Uric acid evidence** | ✅ Direct clinical evidence (CSPPT, meta-analysis) | ❌ Not separately studied for urate |
| **Recommendation** | Use for uric acid reduction | Use for general health if MTHFR variant |
