# DSHEA Compliance Guidelines

All output from this research agent MUST comply with the Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act (DSHEA) of 1994. Supplements are NOT drugs. They cannot be marketed with claims that they diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.

## Permitted: Structure/Function Claims

Structure/function claims describe the role of a nutrient or dietary ingredient in maintaining normal body structure or function. These are allowed under DSHEA.

### Acceptable Language Patterns

- "Supports healthy immune function"
- "Helps maintain normal blood sugar levels already within the normal range"
- "May support cardiovascular health"
- "Plays a role in bone mineral density"
- "Contributes to antioxidant defense"
- "Supports the body's natural inflammatory response"
- "Helps maintain cognitive function"
- "Supports healthy energy metabolism"

### Key Qualifiers to Use

- "may support"
- "helps maintain"
- "plays a role in"
- "contributes to"
- "is involved in"
- "supports the body's natural..."
- "already within the normal range"

## Prohibited: Disease Claims

Disease claims are statements that a product can diagnose, treat, cure, mitigate, or prevent a disease. These make a supplement an unapproved drug under FDA regulations.

### NEVER Use Language Like

- "Treats diabetes"
- "Cures arthritis"
- "Prevents cancer"
- "Reduces risk of heart disease"
- "Lowers cholesterol" (implies treating hypercholesterolemia)
- "Anti-inflammatory" (implies treating inflammation as a disease)
- "Fights infection"
- "Heals wounds faster"
- "Reduces tumor size"
- "Manages hypertension"

### Borderline Cases — How to Reframe

| ❌ Disease Claim | ✅ Structure/Function Claim |
|---|---|
| Lowers blood pressure | Supports healthy blood pressure already within the normal range |
| Reduces inflammation | Supports the body's natural inflammatory response |
| Prevents osteoporosis | Supports bone mineral density |
| Treats depression | Supports positive mood and emotional well-being |
| Boosts immunity to fight colds | Supports healthy immune function |
| Lowers blood sugar | Helps maintain blood sugar levels already within the normal range |
| Reduces cholesterol | Supports healthy lipid metabolism |
| Prevents cognitive decline | Supports cognitive function and mental clarity |

## Required Disclaimers

When reporting findings that will be used in product labeling or marketing:

> *These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.*

## Evidence Reporting Rules

1. **Always cite the PubMed source** — format as `https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/{PMID}/`
2. **Report what the study found** — do not extrapolate to disease claims
3. **Use hedging language** — "The study observed...", "Results suggest...", "Evidence indicates..."
4. **Distinguish between in-vitro, animal, and human studies** — note the study type
5. **Note study limitations** — sample size, duration, population studied
6. **Do not conflate biomarker changes with disease outcomes** — a change in CRP levels is not the same as "treating inflammation"

## Biomarker Reporting

When discussing biomarkers:

- Report reference ranges factually
- Describe ingredient effects on biomarkers using structure/function language
- Example: "Zinc supplementation has been associated with supporting healthy C-reactive protein levels" (NOT "Zinc reduces CRP" or "Zinc treats inflammation")

## Ingredient Dosing

When reporting effective doses from literature:

- Cite the specific study and dose used
- Note that individual needs may vary
- Do not make therapeutic dose recommendations
- Frame as: "In the referenced study, participants received X mg/day"
- Never say "take X mg to treat Y"

## Red Flags — Stop and Reframe If You Write:

- Any form of "treat", "cure", "prevent", "diagnose", "mitigate"
- "Clinically proven to..."
- "Doctor recommended for..."
- "FDA approved" (supplements are NOT FDA approved)
- Comparative claims against drugs ("as effective as ibuprofen")
- Implied disease claims through symptom language ("relieves joint pain" implies treating arthritis)
